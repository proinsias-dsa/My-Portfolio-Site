OneView Healthcare App Design

Design Challenge Concept

OneView Healthcare, who provide a healthcare companion app for hospitals, ran a design competition early in 2016 to find a skilled UI designer. The challenge was to create a UI for one of three target audiences; children, teens or adults.

I chose to target the adult demographic as often these types of solutions fail to engage with older users and offer condesending accessibility for elderly users. I put myself in the position of a patient that would potentially use the OneView solution to identify their needs and wants. Also, I wanted to identify common problems with any recovery process so I took that into consideration too.

From my analysis, I concluded that patients in hospitals normally have a hightened level of stress because they are in unfamiliar surroundings and the care staff don't always have the time to educate them fully on all aspects of their condition. With that in mind I created a dashboard UI that would offer a calming tone and provide vital information to the patient. The patient can view their upcoming appointments and receive alerts for when to take any medication they are on. They can also contact the care staff with a messaging system and listen to music or watch movies to help distract them from their surroundings.

My design won the adult section of the competition and I, along with the other two section winners, was asked to come to OneView Heathcare's Blackrock premises to present my designs to the staff and CEO. The overall winner would recieve a 6 month paid internship. It was a great experience presenting to everyone at OneView and I am happy to of had the opportunity. Unfortuantely I didn't win the overall prize but as a runner-up I recieved a MacBook Air.
